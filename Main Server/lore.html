Polcompball interactive is a community project attempting to simulate the semi-realistic political landscape of the fictional country known as the Republic of Patrium. However, not all is well in this parliamentary republic. President Adams, known for being someone of great compromise, is widely regarded as being the only thing keeping the country together. Economic stagnation and recent news of Adams’ failing health has raised questions as to whether civil war is on the horizon. As a prominent politician of the Republic of Patrium, you join a political party that aligns with the ideology of your choice in order to attempt to take charge of the nation. Will you lead Patrium back into stability and peace? Or will division triumph? The project will officially begin after May 16 and once we have a minimum of 25 members and 3 admins. The members, representing senators in the Patrium Senate, will be presented with weekly scenarios that their party must present a solution for. At the end of the week, the Senate will meet and vote on which idea they think is best. This choice will have consequences on the future of the nation. Depending on how the parties behave the nation can either peacefully select a new party/leader in time for when Adams dies or will dissolve into civil war. The mechanics of a civil war are still to be determined. Ultimately, each party’s goal should be to dominate the political affairs of the country by getting their legislation/ideas passed and ultimately to be in charge of the nation. Most of the project will take place on a discord server that is currently being organized, although we are considering creating a subreddit as well. Good luck!

Background History: The small continent of Jazira was left largely uninhabited by humans for centuries. In the rest of the world, empires of legend would rise and fall, cultures would grow and prosper, but Jazira would remain the same: isolated and devoid of human contact. Only until the 14th century would Arab traders and their slaves inhabit the northern regions of the continent. However, the nearly impenetrable jungles of the north and western coasts of the massive island would prevent them from going any further inland. These trade posts would develop into the first cities and were eventually seized by Portueguese explorers in the 15th century.
This would result in the first introduction of European culture to the landmass, although the Arabic language and its customs would remain relevant for the following centuries. When the very young portuguese king, Sebastião, died in a battle without heirs, in the 16th century, the trade cities became colonies of the Spanish, although this would not last for long. After the Anglo-Spanish War of 1585-1604 ended in English victory, Jazira fell under the dominion of the English. Sir Byron Ashworth would serve as governor of the newly named “Dominion of Patrium” following the English victory. As governor, Ashworth would claim the entire continent in the name of the English crown, although very little of the island was truly under his control. However, Ashworth would still demonstrate himself as a capable leader and under his governance, Patrium would flourish.
The Continent was divided into 12 provinces, each consisting of a particular group, religion, or culture that inhabited the region. To the west were the portueguese provinces of: Selva, Beleza, and Ilha-Phillip. To the north were the Arabic and Sunni provinces of: Jazira, Altijaria, and daw’ alqamar. The remaining 6 provinces: Victoria, Ashworth, Patrium, Thuzland, Osea, and Glaicia, were predominantly of English and Spanish origin. These various groups were prone to constant infighting which would eventually escalate into civil war with the outbreak of the great depression in 1929. After 4 years of fighting, and over a million dead, the continent would be divided into 4. Jazira in the north, the Republic of Selva to the west, and Thuzland and the Republic of Patrium to the South. Thuzland and Patrium would have a particularly serious rivalry even after the end of the civil war. Thuzland had been settled predominantly by Catholics both of English and Spanish origin while Patrium had a majority of English Protestants. (edited)
This tension between the nations can still be felt today and there are often border skirmishes every few years. Today, the nations of Patrium, Jazira, and Selva have relatively friendly relations although it could be said that Thuzland is the only true ally of the Selvan Republic (both are very Catholic).
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Country Status and Government: Jazira, while relatively small with regards to population, is known for its significant wealth in natural resources and minerals. Its economy could be said to be relatively similar to that of the nations of the Arabian Peninsula and Africa. While it is a constitutional republic, there has been increasing tension between the secular population and the increasing Islamic fundamentalists. While many western nations greatly exaggerate the influence of said theocrats, their presence is growing in numbers. (edited)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Selva is one of the few, if only, true successful nations that emerged from the civil war in 1934. It has close ties with both the United Kingdom and Portugal and was even able to use the Second World War as a means of propping its economy back up. Today, it is a relatively wealthy and industrious nation adored by many across the world.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If Selva is the golden child of the island, Thuzland is the awkward and edgy disappointment of the family. Following the civil war in 1934, the government of Thuzland was overthrown by a military coup led by General Samuel Hopkins. A devout Catholic, Hopkins transformed the nation into a Catholic Theocracy with stratocratic elements. Even today, little is known about what truly happens within the nation and it is often referred to as “God’s Hermit Kingdom.” From the stories of those that have escaped the nation, it has been described as a nation wide military-esque Catholic cult with serious human rights abuses and near-totalitarian control over its people. (edited)
